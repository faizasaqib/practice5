Essays are usually written in continuous, flowing, paragraphed text and don't use section headings.
This may seem unstructured at first, but good essays are carefully structured.



Headings can help by keep the reader informed about where they are in the paper:
i.e., headings and subheadings are like directions or signposts that orient the reader.
They may also act as organizing structures for the writer, however: you can always start with headings that you later remove.
